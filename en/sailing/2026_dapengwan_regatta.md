# 1. Race Information

- Race date: 2026/4/18 (Sat)
- Race area: Dapeng Bay, offshore of Qingzhou, Xiaoliuqiu

# 2. Reflection

This was my first time participating in an offshore keelboat race (the Penghu Regatta last time was canceled due to a typhoon). It had also been a long time since I last stepped onto a sailboat, and I learned a lot of new things.

On the Friday one week before the race, we gathered at our elementary school to discuss race details and strategy. The plan was to depart from Dapeng Bay, start upwind (sailing directly into the wind), round Xiaoliuqiu with a port-side rounding, return to the same finish line, and finally go back to Dapeng Bay. As for role assignments, I was responsible for mainsail trimmer and also watching the start flags (the flags are raised/lowered at 5, 4, 1, and 0 minutes before the start; someone needs to observe and report to the helmsman and the timekeeper to support pre-start decisions).

The following Friday, everyone met at Taipei HSR Station. After taking a departure photo together, we took the high-speed rail down to Donggang, Pingtung, and began racing the next day. Before the race, while reading the sailing instructions, we realized the starting line was different from what we had expected: it was a beam-reach start with the wind on starboard. We re-discussed our strategy, and because the rounding direction changed, we wouldn’t be able to fly the spinnaker. In the end, we decided the spinnaker trimmer would take on other responsibilities.

After leaving the harbor, we smoothly hoisted the mainsail and jib and headed toward the starting line and the race committee boat. I focused on watching the flags. Although I made a brief misjudgment at the beginning, we corrected it in time, and the trimmers eased the sails in time as well. In the end, we started successfully about 10–15 seconds after the start signal. After the start, we sailed for about 4–5 hours. When people weren’t on a specific task, everyone cooperated with hiking/ballast. Especially after we learned that the finish line would close 60 minutes after the second-place boat finished—and we might barely make it—everyone worked hard to keep the boat fast. We ultimately crossed the finish line three minutes before it closed, avoiding a DNF.

When the final results came out, we placed 6th, and we were also the last boat to finish. I’m very grateful for the guidance from the teachers on board and for the cooperation with my elementary school classmates. Thanks to that teamwork, we were able to beat some adult crews and achieve such a good result.

The next day, we also successfully relocated the boat to Kaohsiung. Everything went smoothly on the way, and we returned to Taipei in the evening. That concluded the race.
