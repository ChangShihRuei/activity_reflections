Because I studied at an experimental elementary school, I didn’t have regular midterms/finals during elementary school, and I also didn’t review what teachers taught in class in the way many students do. When I entered 7th grade, exam preparation was a brand-new experience for me, and I gradually started exploring how to organize notes and question banks.

My first approach was to use Google Sites to organize notes for all subjects, and use Quizlet to build question banks and memorization materials. The night before an exam, I would read the next day’s notes and review the question bank once. But Google Sites isn’t really a note-taking tool, and some features were limited, so I changed my approach.

My second approach was to use a new note-taking app, Obsidian, to organize notes. Similarly, I used Quizlet for question banks and memorization. The night before an exam, I would read the next day’s notes and review the question bank once, and on the exam day I would review key points in the textbook again before the test. However, during self-study periods at school, this approach sometimes turned into unfocused reviewing, so I changed it again to make school-time review more effective.

My third approach kept the at-home part the same, but at school I used the self-study time after arts/skills final exams (when the course content was already finished) to prepare key points that can’t easily be shown on a computer—for example, a history timeline. Then I prepared fill-in-the-blank questions: for Chinese, I blanked out handwritten content (for example: “In Chinese L7, explain the meaning of ‘曾不吝情去留’”); for social studies and science, I blanked out key points (for example: “List the 5R in Biology 5-3”). That way, during pre-exam self-study periods, I could finish the blanks, correct them, identify what I didn’t know well, and then continue reviewing.

My final approach was almost the same as the previous one, but I exported my Obsidian notes to Google Docs and printed them out, which solved the problem of not being able to use a computer the next day.

I think the last method was the most effective for exam review. In addition, combining it with Google Calendar and Google Tasks helps with good time management, so I can complete all my review before exams.
