## 2. Reflection

This was my second long-distance cycling trip, and it was part of our 6th-grade “grand tour.” We rode from Fengtian Station in Shoufeng, Hualien to Luye, Taitung—145 km in total—completed over three days.

At my elementary school, a “grand tour” is something every student must complete before graduation. It is defined as a journey that is long in time, intense in effort, and deep in cultural experience. We have to choose a theme, plan the itinerary, confirm the route, and then carry it out—an important challenge before graduation. This time, we chose to cycle from Hualien to Taitung, and the whole trip (including travel there and back) lasted five days.

On Day 1, we took the TRA from Taipei Main Station to Fengtian Station. After doing volunteer service at Wu Wei Wu, we stayed overnight. Day 2 was the start of cycling: we rode 30 km, departing from Fengtian Station and heading south along Provincial Highway 9 to Mataian Wetland. There, we experienced making “balago,” played a tag game designed by classmates, and stayed overnight. Day 3 was 55 km: we continued south along Highway 9, turned into Danongdafu Forest Park, then switched to County Road 193. We listened to a guided tour and played outdoor games at Pineapple Park in Fuxing Community, had lunch, continued south, crossed the Yuli Bridge, and stayed overnight in Yuli. Day 4 was 60 km: from Yuli we rode south on Highway 9, ate a bento in Chishang, and continued all the way to Longtian in Luye, where we stayed overnight. On the final day, we took a chartered vehicle to the Luanshan Forest Culture Museum, experienced indigenous life, interviewed the director Aliman, and finally returned to Taipei by TRA.

In the end, I think this was a rare and valuable experience. We were able to plan a trip that truly belonged to us with classmates and teachers, instead of simply following a fixed graduation trip arranged by the school. It also helped us learn how to plan a trip more effectively in the future—and actually carry it out.
