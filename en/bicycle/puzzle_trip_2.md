## 2. Reflection
This was my fourth long-distance cycling trip and my second time participating in the Puzzle Island Cycling Trip. We rode from Suao, Yilan to MRT Guandu Station in Taipei via Provincial Highway 2. The total distance was 180 km over three days. It was also the trip that felt the hardest to ride, with the most climbs and the steepest ups and downs.

On Day 1, we took the Kamalan bus from MRT Technology Building Station to Luodong at night, stayed there for one night, and prepared for the ride the next day.

On Day 2, we departed from Luodong and took a local train to Suao Station, then started cycling for 60 km. Starting from Suao Station, we followed Provincial Highway 2 straight ahead. After stopping for lunch and rest at Toucheng Old Street, we continued straight. Near Shicheng Station, we entered the Old Caoling Tunnel bicycle path and rode toward Fulong, where we stayed overnight.

On Day 3, we rode 65 km. From Fulong, we entered Keelung city along Provincial Highway 2, rode along the edge of Keelung Harbor, then turned onto Zhonghua Road → Wenhua Road → Xiehe Street → Huhai Road and headed toward Waimushan. We had lunch and rested at Waimushan. After lunch, we continued along the coast, returned to Highway 2, and finally climbed uphill for 5–10 minutes at the border between Wanli and Jinshan, where we stayed overnight at a hillside guesthouse.

On the final day, we rode 55 km. Starting from the guesthouse, we returned to Highway 2 and continued straight. In Sanzhi, we turned onto Chexin Road, bypassed the urban area, and returned to Highway 2 again. After reaching Tamsui, we turned from Xinshi Road into Zhongshan North Road, then went into Tamsui Old Street for lunch and rest. After resting, we rode back along the riverside bike path to MRT Guandu Station, ending the trip.

Although the distance of this trip was not as long as some others, we encountered climbs of different sizes at different times—such as the uphill section after leaving Keelung city for the coast, the steep climb near the Jinshan guesthouse, and the continuous rolling hills in Sanzhi caused by past volcanic activity from Yangmingshan. All of these were exhausting. But every uphill is followed by a downhill, and after climbing, descending felt relaxing and enjoyable. In the end, we finished the journey through a series of ups and downs.
