## 2. Reflection
This was my third long-distance cycling trip. Together with my elementary school classmates, I rode from Guandu in Taipei to Yuanlin in Changhua—225 km in total—which was the longest of my three trips. This ride was the first “piece” of the Puzzle Island Cycling Trip, a special program opened for our graduating class by one of our elementary school teachers (who also runs Puzzle Island cycling trips outside of school). After learning about it, I was interested—and since we didn’t have to complete an entire island ride in one go, I decided to join.

We completed the route over three days. On Day 1, we rode 85 km. Starting from MRT Guandu Station, we first rode north to Bali, then headed south along Provincial Highway 61 (the West Coast Expressway). After passing Linkou, we had lunch at a seaside restaurant in Taoyuan, then continued south. We ended the day by turning into the Hsinchu city area near Nanliao Fishing Harbor to rest.

On Day 2, we rode 70 km. We started from Hsinchu, followed Provincial Highway 1 back to the west coast route, and then accidentally took the wrong ramp at Houlong—but fortunately we still reached Baishatun successfully. After lunch at Baishatun Gongtian Temple, we continued along the west coast and later turned onto County Road 132 in Daan District, then rested in Dajia.

On Day 3, we rode another 70 km. We started from Dajia in Taichung, traveled along Provincial Highway 1, connected to Provincial Highway 17 in Qingshui, followed the coastline into Lukang Old Street for lunch, and then after lunch rode along the roads beneath Provincial Highway 76 into Yuanlin. We finished the trip at Yuanlin Train Station.

During the ride, our teacher took a wrong turn a few times, which let us see some different scenery (although it added an extra 5–10 km). Overall, we didn’t encounter many emergencies or accidents, and everyone completed the trip safely. This was the longest trip I’ve done in both single-day distance and total distance. It challenged our stamina, but it also let us see Taiwan’s scenery in more depth—and it was good training for endurance as well.
