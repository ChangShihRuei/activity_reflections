I’ve loved studying buses and transportation since I was little, and on weekends I often went out with my family to ride buses.

I think it started when I was about 3 years old. I began by studying the bus stop signs downstairs from my home. Then, every weekend, I would ride buses with my family to all kinds of places. Before long, I had taken almost every bus route near my home. After that, we started going farther and riding even more routes. I quickly memorized most of Taipei’s bus lines and their routes, could be “quizzed” on them, and even extended that interest into learning the rough layout of Taipei’s roads and gaining a better understanding of the greater Taipei area.

Next, I started researching how bus stops are named. I noticed that different naming styles reflect different eras. Through stop renaming, I gradually understood the importance of naming rules and conventions, and I also discovered a few unusual stops by looking for exceptions to those rules. I also looked into how route terminals are decided: why do some intercity bus routes keep changing their start and end points—could it be related to where their depots are? Some buses also look very similar—are certain bus companies part of the same group?

However, as I grew older and taller, I started getting motion sickness more easily. I also became busier, so I ride buses much less now. Still, even if I don’t take buses out for fun as often, I sometimes look up what’s changed in the world of buses at home.

In the end, I think riding buses has influenced me a lot. It led me to become interested in transportation in Taiwan, and even in other kinds of transport such as the MRT, TRA, and HSR. When traveling around the greater Taipei area, I can quickly suggest the fastest route and guide a group to move efficiently. Also, studying buses helped me learn to read early—I could recognize most commonly used characters by first grade. That’s why I believe buses have had a big impact on me.
